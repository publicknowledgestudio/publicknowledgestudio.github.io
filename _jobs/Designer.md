# Designer — Public Knowledge Studio

## The Role

Public Knowledge Studio is looking for a designer who enjoys working across disciplines and is excited by the evolving nature of design.

We work with early-stage startups, which means every project is different. Some days you might be shaping a brand identity, other days designing a product interface, prototyping a website, or experimenting with motion and AI tools.

The ideal person for this role is self-directed, curious, and comfortable moving between ideation and execution. You'll work closely with one of the studio partners on client projects and will often be responsible for carrying ideas through to a polished outcome.

Experience in **at least two** of the following areas is expected:

- Branding  
- Website design  
- Website development (Framer, Webflow, or code)  
- Motion and video  
- Product design  

This isn’t a traditional design role.

We believe design improves when designers understand the **material they are working with**. For us, that includes code, systems, and increasingly AI. Figma is one tool in the process, but it’s rarely the final destination.

We’re looking for someone curious about how things are actually made — and interested in exploring new ways of designing and producing digital work.

---

## What You'll Do

- Design brand identities, websites, and digital products for early-stage startups  
- Prototype ideas in code using HTML/CSS, frameworks, or AI-assisted tools  
- Collaborate with founders to translate vision into clear, thoughtful design  
- Experiment with new tools and workflows, and help shape how we work as a studio  
- Contribute to internal tools and systems when existing ones fall short  

---

## What We're Looking For

- **2–4 years of experience** in branding, web design, or product design (flexible for the right person)  
- A strong portfolio that demonstrates visual craft and structured thinking  
- Comfort using code as a creative tool — not necessarily as a full-time developer  
- Curiosity about AI, emerging tools, and unconventional design workflows  
- Clear communication and the ability to work independently with startup clients  
- Self-motivation and a desire to continually improve as a designer  

---

## How We Think About Design

We think of design as the practice of shaping systems — not just screens.

Sometimes that means drawing. Sometimes it means writing code. Sometimes it means building tools that didn’t exist before.

Good designers understand the material they’re working with. For digital design, that increasingly means understanding how things are built — technically, structurally, and operationally.

We care about designers who are curious about the **entire lifecycle of making things**, not just the moment when pixels are arranged.

---

## Signals We Look For in Portfolios

Portfolios don’t need to be perfect, but they should show how you think.

Things we love seeing:

- Designers who prototype ideas in code  
- Experiments with AI or unconventional tools  
- Personal projects and self-initiated explorations  
- Evidence of process and reasoning, not just polished outputs  
- Work that shows curiosity about how things are built
